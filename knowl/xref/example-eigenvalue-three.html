Example 2.7.3.

Consider the matrix \(A=\begin{pmatrix} 2 \amp 0 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 2 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 3\end{pmatrix}\text{.}\) The eigenvalues of \(A\) are \(2\) and \(3\) (take this for granted for now). Let’s try to describe all eigenvectors associated to the eigenvalue \(2\text{.}\) This means that we want to solve the system
\begin{equation*} A\colvec{x \\ y \\ z}=2\colvec{x \\ y \\ z}\text{,} \end{equation*}
which is equivalent to the system
\begin{equation*} (A-2\Id_2)\colvec{x \\ y \\ z}= \colvec{0 \\ 0 \\ 0}\text{.} \end{equation*}
Now, \(A-2\Id_2=\begin{pmatrix} 0 \amp 0 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 0\amp 1\end{pmatrix}\text{.}\) The solutions to
\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix} 0 \amp 0 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 0 \amp 0 \\ 0 \amp 0\amp 1\end{pmatrix}\colvec{x \\ y \\ z}=\colvec{0 \\ 0 \\ 0} \end{equation*}
are all the vectors of the form \(\colvec{x \\ y \\ 0}\text{.}\) So, the eigenvectors associated to \(2\) are all the non-zero vectors in \(\colvec{x \\ y \\0}\text{.}\)
🔗
Similarly, we may calculate the eigenvectors associated to the eigenvalue \(3\text{.}\) These are the non-zero solutions to
\begin{equation*} (A-3\Id_2)\colvec{x \\ y \\ z} = \colvec{0 \\ 0 \\ 0}\text{.} \end{equation*}
The solutions to this system are the vectors of the form \(\colvec{0 \\ 0 \\ z}\text{.}\)
